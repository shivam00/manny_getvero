]]> By: Reuben https://www.getvero.com/resources/pricing-strategies-from-drug-dealers-to-make-more-money/#comment-13443 Sun, 08 Dec 2013 16:05:00 +0000 http://blog.getvero.com/?p=1204#comment-13443 Great question. The answer depends on a number of factors, including whether you have the resources to support a huge number of non-paying users. In addition, consider if the audience should pay. For example, people have different expectations of Google as a search user or Google as an advertiser. Google may offer a free credit to get started with advertising, but you are going to pay for that eventually. In other situations, non-paying users are useful if they can create a network effect or you have other ways to monetize them. Don’t count on a large percentage of them upgrading to paid plans. Some will hit usage triggers and upgrade, but many are there simply for the “free”. There’s a huge change in demand between free and non-free. As a general rule of thumb, B2B apps are more likely to be free trial and B2C apps are more likely to be freemium.
