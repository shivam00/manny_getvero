]]> By: John Dubock https://www.getvero.com/resources/remote-work/#comment-14270 Sun, 19 Apr 2015 22:07:00 +0000 http://blog.getvero.com/?p=4398#comment-14270 Having worked remotely fr home for 8 years now, the tools are there to function and get results. Try it for a few hours a week at first, see the challenges, then adapt if its for you. You’ll miss that ‘anger in the office’ at your lazy co workers that bring their wackiness to work. You’ll realize work is not breaking rocks all day, we live with peaks n valley of productivity. You do need an All Star Team around you, fr the local tech guy that recommends the right keyboard to a virtual assistant that appreciates the Skype training once a week. Going simple helps, Chromebooks coupled with a real CRM and a used Nexus 5 allow you to tack on Rapportive, Ebsta, Phonami, Phonedeck, Nimble, Insightly, Base and Ace of Sales and definitely ScheduleOnce or Calendly. Headsets.com is another All Star.

I set appointments in 8 time zones, 15hrs a day on call 24/7, and incoming emails are amazed at a simple, humane reply, then decide on stock email replies, automated email campaigns depending on the response.

Coffee shops and libraries are getting PACKED with telecommuting workers, so expect to get creative to find new spots to work smartly. Car dealerships, McDonalds, Subways, can work if its quiet. Entire cities are wired, like St. George, UT and Stevenson, WA.

Life isn’t quiet, and expect a ton of rude idiots talking so loud even though they can see you hugging a phone. Get to know the larger coffee shops in town and tip them well, then start researching the giant database of new co working hotel lobbies, spare offices etc near you as a professional break.

New cities to work in sound cool, but expect to hustle to find consistent power, Net access. Joining LinkedIn barely helps your career, all the social apps are so busy selling something you have to flip the equation. Get your own Private Group on LinkedIn, delete any losers in FB. Twitter can be a resource of ideas, tips, but it too gets noisy with ads.

Remote is the future, true your phone ties you down, but if you’re a responsive, motivated worker you win.
